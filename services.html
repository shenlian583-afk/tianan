Co-Manufacturing Models

Three commercial models for design studio partnerships.

Start with studio direct order by default, upgrade to tripartite governance for complex programs, or use referral commission for lead-only collaboration. All models leverage scalable Dongguan factory access under one engineering team.

Model Matrix

When to use each model and what each party controls.

Studio Direct Order (Default)

Best for most projects. The design studio places PO and remains the client-facing lead.

Outputs: white-label engineering execution, DFM, validation package, pilot transfer plan.

Tripartite Collaboration

Best for tooling-heavy, certification-heavy, or high-capex projects requiring shared governance.

Outputs: three-party role map, shared milestone approvals, risk and payment checkpoints.

Referral Commission

Best when a studio introduces opportunities but does not join project management.

Outputs: lead registration, qualified referral definition, commission statement and payment schedule.

Execution Rhythm

Commercial and delivery checkpoints that keep projects predictable.

Checkpoint 01

Intake + Model Selection

Confirm project stage, risk level, and whether direct order or tripartite governance is required.

Checkpoint 02

Scope + Boundary Lock

Define front-end design boundary, engineering scope, approval owners, and communication channels.

Checkpoint 03

Stage-gated Delivery

Run EVT/DVT/PVT milestones with explicit sign-off criteria and controlled scope changes.

Checkpoint 04

Pilot + Ramp Handoff

Release manufacturing package and quality controls with owner-specific post-launch responsibilities.

Deliverables By Default
