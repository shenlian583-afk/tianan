Frequently Asked Questions

Operational answers before you commit time and budget.

This page covers fit criteria, delivery speed assumptions, cost transparency, IP handling, and collaboration models.

General

Who we are and which projects are a strong fit.

Is Geniotek a design firm, a trading company, or a factory?

We are a D2M integrated development partner combining design, engineering, prototyping, and manufacturing integration.

What projects are prioritized?

We prioritize innovation-led products with clear differentiation potential and long-term brand ambition.

Can you handle both electronics and mechanical systems?

Yes. Electromechanical integration is one of our core strengths across consumer and utility categories.

Do you support new category creation?

Yes. We often help teams define category logic and a manufacturable MVP path at the same time.

What information should we prepare before first discussion?

Concept summary, target user, expected price range, any sketches/3D files, and existing research if available.

How long is the initial feasibility review?

The kickoff review is typically structured around a 30-minute expert session with follow-up written direction.

Can we engage only one stage, not full-cycle?

Yes. Services are modular and can be scoped by your current stage and internal capability.

Is remote collaboration possible?

Yes. We operate with mixed collaboration: remote decision cadence plus on-site supplier coordination where needed.

Speed + Cost

How timeline and cost advantages are achieved in practice.

What does the “average 60% speed increase” compare against?

It compares against traditional fragmented workflows where design, engineering, and manufacturing decisions happen sequentially across separate teams.

Why can integrated workflows move faster?

Because manufacturability, sourcing, and engineering constraints are part of early decisions, reducing redesign loops later.

How is cost transparency managed?

By sharing material and process tradeoffs early, and validating supply assumptions continuously instead of waiting until final quotation.

Is the 40% cost optimization guaranteed?

No single percentage is guaranteed for every case. It is an observed average range from suitable project profiles and disciplined execution.

Risk + Quality

Assurance, compliance, and protection topics.

How do you protect our IP?

Through confidentiality agreements, access segmentation, and controlled sharing of critical technical data during supplier coordination.

Do you support CE/FCC/RoHS certification pathways?

Yes. Compliance requirements are integrated into design and production planning, not treated as a late-stage add-on.

How do you ensure prototype-to-production fidelity?

By defining tolerance/material baselines and enforcing them through pilot checkpoints and QC standards.

What happens if pilot issues appear?

We use a structured issue escalation and corrective loop before approving scale ramp decisions.
